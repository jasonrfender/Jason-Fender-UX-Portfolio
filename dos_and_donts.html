The Do's and Don't when Traveling

Do

Have packages sent to front desk

It's best practice to have any packages sent to your residence while abroad to location where they can be received without you having to sign for them on the spot. These locations could be a front desk at a hotel, a P.O. Box or a mailbox.

Don't

Accept packages sent to your room

It is unwise to except a package in a location that you are unfamiliar with your surroundings. You also don't want to except any packages that show up unexpectedly This could be a ruse that is used to conduct a robbery or worse, a kidnapping.

Do

Randomize your commute

Randomizing your commute is always a wise security practice when traveling abroad, do your best to take alternative routes to your location. Do your best to not set patterns whether you are walking, biking or driving to your location. Another pattern to be aware of is your dining habits, try to rotate your time and location where you are eating out at.

Don't

Set patterns

Don't take the same route to your location everyday. This is a good way for someone to observe a pattern that could be used to conduct nefarious activities against you. The same goes for the times and places you go when you're abroad, don't consistently go to the same restaurant at the same time everyday because this could easily be observed and used for negative reasons.

Do

Travel in numbers

Always do your best to travel in numbers while abroad. At a minimum, at least travel in pairs. You make yourself a harder target for bad actors when you're not alone. If a bad actor was observing your movements, they would be a lot less likely to try to take advantage of someone that they knew wasn't going to be by themselves.

Don't

Travel by yourself

Don't travel by yourself because this can make you a target of oppurtunity. If you have to travel by yourself, always have a plan in place for how you will get from point A to point B. Give someone your itinerary so that they are aware of your timeliine and organize check-ins if necessary.

Do

Talk about the local soccer/football team

This is a very generalized topic to talk about. But if all other small talk fails, talking about a local soccer/football team where you're traveling abroad is always a safe topic. Talking about other popular sports, music, food or even theatre is good topics to focus on when making small talk with locals.

Don't

Talk about politics

This is the golden rule even when traveling within the US, so when you're traveling abroad it's best to practice this rule as your number one rule. Talking about politics in a foreign country can be a recipe for disaster and it's a subject that should be avoided at all cost.

Do

Know the local laws to the best of your ability

It's a good practice to familiarize yourself with some of the local laws before you arrive in the area that you're traveling to. Although some of the local laws may seem odd compared to what you are used to back home, it is best to follow them so you can avoid interactions with the local authorites.

Don't

Assume the law will be the same as they are back home

Never assume that what you can get away with back home will be the same when you're traveling abroad. For instance in Europe jaywalking is not conducted and is usually always enforced. Traffic laws while driving in another country can be almost polar opposites than what they are in the US. Speed cameras are heavily used and the fines can be extreme to say the least.

Do

Explore and have fun

This is of course a given, when traveling abroad you should always do your best to enjoy yourself and have as much fun as possible. This is of course have as much fun as possible, but within reason. Always use your best judgement when it comes to pushing the limits of what people consider fun and what some people might consider wreckless.

Don't

Visit places that could be restricted to visitors

Always do your best to be familiar with any areas that may be off limits to people and might especially restricted to out of country visitors. It is not uncommon for countries outside the US to have what is called black listed or restricted zones. It's best to be aware of these areas and avoid them at all cost.
