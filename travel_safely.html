How to Travel Abroad Safely as a Student

Have a Plan

Before traveling it is always best to have a plan when it comes to what-if scenarios that require one. If there is a natural disaster what do you do, where do you go? If you have to evacuate the country, how will you do so? With Covid being a serious health issue currently. What happens if you catch Covid and have to quarantine in place? These are all descent what-if scenarios that you should have a rough plan for.

Use a Travel App

A good travel app is always a valuable tool have on your smartphone. If you can get one that works offline that's even better, although that can be a tall order to fill. This will allow you to use the app in a country that might not have the most reliable cell phone network. If you have the money to pay for a travel app that gives you location specific information and realtime updates, this can be extremely valuable informatino when traveling abroad.

Reliable Communications are Key

As I mentioned in the previous section, the area you are traveling in might not have the best cell phone network. Before you make your trip it is best to know how reliable your smartphone and service will be in the area you will be visiting. If your service is going to be too unreliable, it is a good idea to buy a local prepaid flip phone that will get service just about any place you go in the country.

Be Aware of your Surroundings

This topic can be a little difficult to perform without exhausting oneself from constantly being in a state of hightened awareness. But it is always good to know things like where the nearest exit is to your location, where the shelter inplace location is for natural disasters and even where civil unrest or politcal rallies may be held that day near your location. You should also know where the closest US embassy or consulate is to your location. If you are in a country that doesn't have either of these, it is best to know where the nearest emabassy/consulate is that can help you or communicate with the US on your behalf. Some examples of these countries are Canada and Switzerland. These examples are just a few things that you can take into consideration and are a drop in the bucket when it comes to being area of your surroundings.

Educate Yourself before Traveling

This is a topic that is always going to be broad in nature. You can spend days and nights doing research on an area that you're getting ready to travel to. Somethings I always look at when I'm traveling is the health conditions of the area, should I drink the local tap water, what do I look for when dining out. What is the crime like in the area I'll be visiting, what is the most common crime perpetrated against tourist? Take pickpocketing for instance, the tactics that some petty theifs use in one country might not be used in the next. So how you will protect youself from these tactics will vary.

Carry Local Currency

Do your best to carry various forms of currency that can be used in the area you're traveling in. It's possible that credit cards may not be accepted everywhere you go, so it's best to have cash as a backup for your credit card. It is also a good idea to be aware of the fine print when withdrawing money from local ATMs, some machines can detect a debit card that is out of country and will change the fees and raise the dollar amounts that can be withdrawn.

Inspect your Vehicle

The idea of inspecting your vehicle can vary by your location and is purely a suggestion. You will want to start by making sure that you vehicle is still locker and that nothing has been tampered with, like the gas cap cover and the trunk. You'll also want to inspect the vehicle to make sure nothing was slide under it or attached to it. Here in the US human traffickers were using a number of zipties to keep a unknowing bystander from being able to get into their care. While they were distracted by trying to get the zipties off the door handle, a vehicle would come in behind them and people would job out in order to grab them and quickly throw them in the vehicle and drive off without anybody seeing anything.

Learn about the Culture

Learning about the culture before you travel to a location outside the US is very important. You will want to learn the Do's/Don'ts of a specific culture. In some cultures this is disrespectful to make direct eye contact with the men and even women in some cases. It can be sign of disrespect to show the bottom of your shoes in others. It's always good to learn as much about the culture before and during your trip, it will make things a lot easier when you get to your location and you'll be much more enlightened when you get to experience the culture first hand.

Safeguard your Hard to Replace Valuables

If at all possible do your best to safeguard your valuables, especially your passport. If your hotel room or the place you are staying at has a safe that is reliable this is always going to be a good place to store your valuables. If you don't have access to something like this, it is always good to keep a good lock on hand when you travel because a lot place have closets with a latch that can be locked. If neither of these options are available then the next best option for securing your valuables will be with a travel wallet/pouch that can be stored/hidden on yourself.
